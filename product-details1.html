NUT 24° CONE

DIN-Connector

The nut 24° cone DIN-connector is a type of bite-ring connector that uses a 24-degree cone to create a tight and leak-proof seal with a tube. It is commonly used in hydraulic and pneumatic systems, as well as in other applications where a reliable and leak-proof connection is required. as well as in other applications where a reliable and leak-proof connection is required. The nut 24° cone DIN-connector is a two-piece fitting that consists of the connector nut and a matching cutting ring. The cutting ring is made of soft steel or stainless steel and has a 24-degree cone shape. The nut has a corresponding 24-degree cone-shaped internal surface. When the nut is tightened onto the cutting ring, the cutting ring bites into the tube, creating a tight and leak-proof seal. The 24-degree cone shape provides a strong and stable connection, even under high pressure and vibration. The nut 24° cone DIN-connector is available in a variety of sizes to accommodate different tube diameters. It is also available in different materials, such as brass, stainless steel, and nickel-plated brass. Here are some of the advantages of using a nut 24° cone DIN-connector: Leak-proof seal Strong and stable connection Available in a variety of sizes and materials Easy to install Reusable Here is a disadvantage of using a nut 24° cone DIN-connector: The cutting ring can be damaged if overtightened Overall, the nut 24° cone DIN-connector is a reliable and versatile type of bite-ring connector that is well-suited for a wide range of applications.
