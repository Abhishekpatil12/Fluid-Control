UNION ELBOW 24° CONE ENDS

DIN-Connector

A "Union Elbow 24° Cone Ends" refers to a specific type of hydraulic fitting that combines the features of a union, an elbow, and 24° cone ends. Let's break down the components: Union Elbow: This indicates that the fitting is both a union and an elbow. A union allows for the easy disconnection of pipes or tubes, while an elbow provides a change in direction for the fluid or gas flow. 24° Cone Ends: The 24° cone ends, as mentioned before, refers to the specific shape of the sealing surface on the fitting. This shape, often combined with O-ring seals, is common in hydraulic systems for creating secure and leak-tight connections. In practical terms, a "Union Elbow 24° Cone Ends" could be used in a hydraulic system where there's a need for a bend in the piping or tubing, and the ability to disconnect the components easily for maintenance or repairs. The 24° cone ends ensure a reliable and tight seal when the fitting is connected. The use of specific fittings like this is common in applications where precision and reliability are crucial, such as in hydraulic machinery, aerospace systems, or other industrial settings. Always refer to the manufacturer's specifications and guidelines for the proper installation and use of these fittings in a given system.
